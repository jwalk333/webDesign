The record label's “No Limit Soldier” moniker, and the infamous military tank used as the company logo, pays homage to Master P's father, grandfather, younger brother, and other relatives who served in the military. Here, the young rapper is seen showing off the popular logo that dangles from his chain.

At the peak of his career, the famous rapper decided to start playing basketball professionally. In 1998, Master P joined the Fort Wayne Fury in the CBA. Despite not being a standout athlete, Master P got a chance in the NBA. His career in the prestigious basketball league began in January 1999, when he joined the Charlotte Hornets' training camp roster.

In 2017, Rap Snacks CEO James Lindsay began collaborating with Percy “Master P” Miller to expand the product line into foods such as noodles, breakfast cereals, and rice with the creation of P&J Foods, a partnership between the two. Miller is a limited partner in Rap Snacks.

The rapper and business man expanded his portfolio with the creation of MoneYatti. Moneyatti is a unique blend of luxury and streetwear, creating a bridge between the two styles. The brand is known for its bold designs, which are inspired by the street culture and urban lifestyle.

On June 28, 2021, Mayor LaToya Cantrell presented Percy "Master P" Miller with the key to the city. He has worked tirelessly to provide for the less fortunate and aid with programs directed towards stopping gun violence in New Orleans.

The Colonel of The Tank, Master P, gathered his No Limit Soldiers, such as Mystikal and Mia X, for a 20-year anniversary reunion tour performing some of the greatest hits from the No Limit catalog.
